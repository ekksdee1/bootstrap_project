Italian History in the United States

In the late 1800s, there was a mass emigration to the United States from Italy, in which about 5 million people arrived in the states. With all of these people, the Italian culture was brought to the states, and along with their culture came their food!

The Italians brought pizza, which is why pizza is so common in the U.S. today, even if it is Americanized pizza. It quickly became a hit in the early 1900s, as an inexpensive, hot, and delicious food. The Italians also brought the long sandwich to the states, and that evolved into the well-known sub. You could even make an argument that Subway only exists because the Italians brought the long sandwich to the U.S!

Italian ingredients were brung to the U.S. so that they could be readily available at a later date though; it wasn't until the Mediterranean Food revolution that ingredients such as olive oil and dried pasta were easily available to the public outside of Italy. It's also important to mention that a reputation was made for Italian restaurants, in that they were romantic and classy restaurants where a couple could go to eat. This is seen even today, where restaurant chains such as Olive Garden are somewhat classy.
